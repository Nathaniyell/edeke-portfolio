E-commerce Skincare Website (2022 & 2024)

In 2022, I embarked on a journey to design an e-commerce website for a skincare brand. As a UI/UX designer, my focus was to create a platform that could not only sell products but also educate and engage users about skincare routines tailored to their needs. However, at the time, I did not fully consider key design principles such as alignment, hierarchy, color theory, and font choices. In 2024, I revisited the website, applying a deeper understanding of these concepts to create a more polished and engaging user experience. This case study explores my thought process, challenges, and solutions across both versions.

Project Timeline

First Design: 2022

Redesign: 2024

Understanding the Brand and Users (2022)

Before diving into design, I invested time in understanding the skincare brand’s identity and its core audience. The target demographic for the website was primarily women aged 20-45, who are health-conscious, tech-savvy, and eager to invest in skincare solutions that suit their specific skin types and issues.

User Research:

I conducted surveys and interviews with potential users to discover pain points and desires in online skincare shopping:
Pain Points:

  • Users struggled with overwhelming product options.
  • They lacked guidance in choosing products for their skin concerns.
Desires:
  • A seamless and personalized shopping experience.
  • Information on skincare ingredients.

Based on this research, I knew the website had to be simple, educational, and visually appealing to users. However, I lacked a strong understanding of how to apply alignment, hierarchy, colors, and font pairings, which later became key areas for improvement in 2024.

First Version (2022) – Initial Approach and Design Decisions

User Research: Goals:

The main goals were to create a user-friendly interface and smooth navigation for product exploration and purchasing. In this version, I emphasized:


  • Minimalistic Layout: I focused on a clean and minimalist aesthetic to avoid overwhelming users.
  • Simple Navigation: I kept the menu straightforward, with categories like Body lotion, Body wash, Body scrub, Organic oils, based on popular product types.

However, in this first version, I had limited understanding of key UI/UX principles. I did not fully consider alignment, which led to inconsistent spacing in the layout. My use of hierarchy was not well-defined, making it difficult for users to quickly navigate the content. Color choices were neutral, but I had not yet mastered how to use them to guide attention or create contrast. Similarly, my font selection was simplistic and did not create a strong visual identity for the brand.
