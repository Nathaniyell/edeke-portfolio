SmartFunds - Fintech App Design

Project Overview

  • Project Name:   SmartFunds
  • Role:   UI/UX Designer/Researcher
  • Duration:  8 weeks
  • Tools Used:  Figma
  • Project Type:  Self-initiated, skill enhancement project

Project Goal

The goal of this project was to design an intuitive and inclusive fintech app interface that seamlessly enables users to:

  • Send and receive money
  • Access financial education
  • Explore investment and loan options
  • Manage bill payments
  • Set savings goals
  • Interact with AI features
  • Navigate the app in multiple languages

    • All of these features were built to offer a user-friendly and engaging financial experience.

Problem Statement

The challenge was to create a seamless, user-friendly platform that:

  1. Simplifies financial transactions for all types of users.
  2. Provides accessible financial education to empower users.
  3. Offers investment and loan opportunities in an easy-to-understand format.
  4. Supports bill payments and savings goals management effortlessly.
  5. Incorporates multilingual support to enhance inclusivity across various regions.

Research & Discovery

To address the needs of a broad user base, I categorized my research into three key generational segments: Gen Z, Gen Y (Millennials), and Gen X. Each generation has different financial behaviors and needs, which influenced the design approach

  1. Gen Z (Born 1997–2012) Tech-savvy and mobile-first, Gen Z users expect fast, seamless transactions and AI-driven interactions. They are more likely to experiment with financial tools like cryptocurrency and digital wallets.
    • Key Insight: Gen Z prefers clean, modern interfaces with minimal friction. They respond well to interactive elements like AI chatbots and are interested in financial education that incorporates gamification.
  2. Gen Y / Millennials (Born 1981–1996) Millennials value convenience and prefer apps that allow them to manage their money on the go, with emphasis on budgeting tools and investment options.
    • Key Insight: They are highly interested in investment and savings tools, and they seek financial advice to plan for long-term goals like homeownership. Clear navigation and data visualization are key to engaging them.
  3. Gen X (Born 1965–1980) Gen X prefers more traditional banking tools but is increasingly embracing digital financial platforms. They value security and detailed information before making financial decisions.
    • Key Insight: Gen X users tend to need more detailed guidance, especially with loans and investment tools. They also prefer in-depth educational content over quick tips, focusing on long-term financial planning.
  4. Competitive Analysis: I studied existing fintech apps like Revolut, Chime, and N26 to understand their strengths and identify areas for improvement in my design, considering the preferences of each generational segment. Research link

  2. Information Architecture

    3. Creating a clear and effective information architecture (IA) was a core part of the design process to ensure users could navigate the app’s features with ease. The IA was designed to prioritize accessibility and reduce friction, based on generational needs.

    The IA was organized as follows:

    • Home Screen: Quick access to actions like sending money, viewing balances, and recent transactions.
    • Savings & Investment: A section for users to set savings goals, view investment options, and monitor their progress.
    • Bill Payments: Designed for managing utility payments, airtime top-ups, and recurring subscriptions, taking inspiration from Kuda's simplicity in bill payments.
    • Financial Education: A hub that features articles, tutorials, and interactive tools for financial literacy, with personalized content for different generational needs.
    • AI-Assisted Support: Accessible throughout the app to provide real-time help with tasks like sending money, investment guidance, or bill payment.
    • Multilingual Support: Integrated seamlessly to ensure inclusivity for a diverse user base.

    The structure of the IA reflected user priorities, allowing easy access to key financial features, while ensuring simplicity and consistency across the app.

Font Selection

For the typography, I chose Poppins as the primary font for its modern, clean, and geometric appearance. Here's why:

  • Legibility: As a fintech app, clarity is crucial for financial transactions, and Poppins offers great readability across various screen sizes, from mobile to desktop.
  • Aesthetic Balance: Poppins balances both professionalism and approachability, which is important for creating trust in financial products. Its rounded letterforms give a friendly feel without compromising on a sense of security.
  • Versatility: With multiple weight variants (light, regular, bold), Poppins allowed me to create visual hierarchy. For example, Bold was used for headings like "Send Money" or "Your Balance", while Regular kept body text easy on the eyes. The Light variant softened secondary details, contributing to a clean, uncluttered look.

Color Scheme

Primary Color: #008080 (Teal)

I chose teal as the primary color to reflect stability, trust, and clarity, which are key attributes users seek in financial apps. The color has an interesting psychological appeal:

  • Trust & Dependability: Teal, a blend of blue and green, combines the calming effect of blue (which signifies trust, professionalism) and the balance of green (associated with money, growth, and prosperity). This choice conveys that users can rely on SmartFunds to manage their finances securely and confidently.
  • Approachability: Teal stands out but is not overwhelming. It offers a modern, refreshing feel that differs from the more corporate tones of blue used by traditional banks, creating a welcoming, human-centered experience.

Accent Color: #FF4500 (OrangeRed)

The accent color, a vibrant OrangeRed, was selected for specific interactive elements and CTAs (call-to-actions) like “Send” and “Pay Now.”
Here’s why this color works well:

  • Urgency & Energy: OrangeRed is energetic and draws attention without being harsh. It evokes urgency but remains warm and friendly, making users feel motivated to complete tasks without feeling pressured.
  • Contrast: The orange-red against teal creates a strong contrast that naturally guides users to key actions. This visual hierarchy ensures users immediately notice what they need to do next, minimizing confusion or hesitation.

Usability Testing

  1. Testing Objectives

    2. The main focus of testing was to evaluate:

    • Ease of navigation
    • Clarity in financial education content
    • Accessibility of features like AI and multilingual support
    • Overall satisfaction with the user experience
  2. Key Findings and Improvements
    • Gen Z Feedback: Users from Gen Z appreciated the clean, modern interface and the AI assistant. However, they requested more visual gamification elements in the financial education section.
    • Action Taken: Introduced interactive quizzes and progress trackers in the education section.
    • Millennial Feedback: Millennials loved the savings and investment tools but suggested adding reminders for bill payments.
    • Action Taken: Added notifications and reminders for upcoming bill payments and savings goals.
    • Gen X Feedback: Gen X users wanted more in-depth guidance on loans and investments, along with more visual contrast to make information easier to digest.
    • Action Taken: Created detailed guides and comparisons in the loan section and adjusted contrast for readability.
