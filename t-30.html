Transforming T30: A User-Centric Redesign through Thoughtful UI/UX Design

Project Overview

T30 is a non-denominational Christian platform with daily devotionals and collective prayer times designed to help you build a consistent devotional life. Since its debut in 2020, T30's user base has grown into thousands, allowing people from all over the world to connect, pray and read the word daily.

Just a heads-up: I'm not part of T30, and the opinions in this case study are all mine. Unlike the T30 designers, I don't have complete access to the user data that shaped their current design, the font type, images and the specific elements they used for their design. So, this case study isn't all-encompassing, and I'm not proposing T30 should switch to my redesign.

My goals for the redesign:

  • To facilitate a more engaging and seamless experience when it comes to devotion and staying connected
  • To design a more personable and intuitive user interface
  • To design through user empathy (HCD)

My goals for my own personal development:

  • Learn how to conduct and analyze user research, create flow charts and wireframes, design and prototype through Figma
  • Complete my first redesign project from start to finish while sticking to design principles

The roles I assumed during the process of building this redesign:

  • UI/UX Designer
  • User Researcher
  • Product Designer
  • Data Analyst

Problem Statement

While the current app design serves its purpose, it lacks key elements that could significantly enhance user engagement and community interaction. Users have expressed a desire for features such as testimonials, a community space, a journaling platform, and bookmark functionality, and a voice reader functionality to enrich their experience. To create a more vibrant and user-friendly platform, the redesign aims to seamlessly integrate these components, fostering a stronger sense of community and personal connection for believers.

Target Audience Demographics

Among the 10 T30 users I interviewed, there were 4 males and 6 females. The age of the male users ranged from 25 to 31 years old, while the female users ranged from 22 to 30 years old. 90% are working class.

To make this even more interesting, 30% of the users I interviewed ranked T30 in their Top 3 Most Used Application. Additionally, 50% of the people sampled uses T30 everyday.

Feature #2: Community of Believers

After thorough user research, a notable finding surfaced: 20% of our users voiced a keen interest in having a community hub within the app. Understanding the value of nurturing connections and communal interactions among believers, we introduced the 'Community' feature. This fresh inclusion transcends traditional testimonials, establishing an interactive platform where users can share personal narratives, participate in enriching conversations, offer mutual support, and collectively enhance their faith journey.

Following our user research, a common sentiment emerged among our users: they desired a space within the app where they could connect with fellow believers and share their experiences, insights, and support. One user expressed this desire, stating, "I love T30 for its daily devotionals, but having a community space within the app would be amazing. Connecting with fellow believers could make the experience even richer, allowing us to share insights and support each other on our faith journeys." In this section, we'll delve into how I made my design decisions, while prioritizing the needs of my end users

Design Process - Feature #2

A picture of some sketches

During the brainstorming phase, I strategically considered the optimal placement for integrating the community feature, ensuring its immediate visibility. After careful deliberation, I concluded that the most effective approach was to substitute the 'Give' icon with the 'Community' icon. To streamline the interface and prevent overcrowding, I introduced the hamburger menu, thereby replacing the profile icon. This modification not only accommodated the inclusion of both the profile and give features but also alleviated clutter, creating additional space for the integration of other features under the 'More' section.

I thought of having the T30 team create the communities instead of letting users do it. This way, we prevent the app from having too many or irrelevant groups. Users can easily join any community of their choice without the hassle of navigating through numerous options

More option

Feature #3: A journal

Drawing from insights gathered during user research, it became evident that 10% of our users expressed a strong interest in having a journaling feature integrated into the app. Understanding the significance of personal reflection, we are introducing Feature #3: A Journal. This innovative addition not only offers users a dedicated space for introspection but also enables them to record personal reflections or prayers inspired by the devotionals. One user enthusiastically shared their thoughts, stating, “It will be awesome to jot down personal reflections or prayers inspired by the devotionals.” Let's delve into how this feature elevates the user experience and directly caters to the articulated need of our community.

I came up with an easy-to-use design for the journal, making it simple for users to add, save, and share their notes in the app effortlessly.

Feature #4: Ability to Bookmark

In response to the needs of our user base, where 30% of users expressed a desire for a convenient way to revisit meaningful moments within the app, we have introduced Feature #4: Ability to Bookmark. This innovative addition grants users the power to effortlessly bookmark content, enabling them to revisit and cherish moments that resonate with them the most. Let's explore how this feature enhances the user experience, catering to the desires of our end users.

Design Process - Feature #4

I devised a user-friendly solution by incorporating a bookmark icon directly into the devotional page. This intuitive addition allows users to effortlessly access and utilize the bookmark functionality, enhancing their overall experience within the app. All bookmarked items are conveniently grouped under the 'More' icon, a decision that not only accommodated the bookmarked items effectively but also opens up space for the inclusion of other features within the menu, contributing to a more streamlined and versatile app interface

Bookmark Feature

Feature #5: Voice Reader

In response to the needs expressed by our users, where 10% highlighted a desire for improved accessibility, we are excited to introduce Feature #5: Voice Reader. This innovative addition integrates a dedicated voice reader icon into the devotional feature, enabling spiritual content to be accessible to all. Acknowledging the significance of designing for accessibility and inclusivity, this feature not only enhances engagement for those who prefer listening but also serves as a valuable tool for individuals with visual impairments who may struggle with reading. By embracing inclusivity, this feature ensures that the devotional content can be enjoyed by a diverse audience, fostering a sense of community and belonging.

Design Strategy

The collective insights gathered informed a user-centric design strategy aimed at addressing specific user needs and concerns. Feature enhancements were prioritized based on both quantitative and qualitative findings.

Moreover, a continuous feedback loop was established with users throughout the design iteration process, ensuring that their voices remained integral to shaping the final product. The design strategy, therefore, not only addresses current user expectations but also positions T30 for adaptability in the ever-evolving landscape of user preferences.

Rationale Behind Design Choices

User-Centered Approach

Think of it as creating a perfect playlist personalized just for you. I took a user-centered approach, making sure the testimony section resonates with what users truly need and expect. It's not just design; it's a tailor-made experience crafted with your preferences in mind

Visual Hierarchy

Imagine crafting a visual story where every section has its own spotlight. That's what I did with testimonials. I strategically placed them, creating a visual roadmap for easy reading and maximum impact. It's about ensuring each testimonial has its moment in the spotlight for a seamless and impactful user experience.

Consistency with Branding

I made sure the testimony section not only looked good but also felt like it belonged. By keeping things in sync with the brand's style – colors, fonts, and all that jazz – the section seamlessly blends into the brand's vibe. It's like giving the audience a visual hug that's both familiar and stylish.

Visual Elements

I utilized annotations to highlight key features within the testimony section. These subtle visual cues serve as labels, ensuring users don't overlook the design gems we've carefully crafted

Considerations for Accessibility, Ensuring Inclusivity in Design
Color Contrast Retention:

While the original color scheme remained unchanged to maintain brand identity, efforts were made to retain optimal color contrast for enhanced readability. This ensures that users with visual impairments can easily distinguish between text and background elements, promoting a more accessible reading experience

Text and Typography Adaptations

The typography system underwent subtle adjustments to accommodate a wider range of users. Font sizes were carefully chosen to enhance readability, and consideration was given to font styles that cater to users with varying visual abilities. This ensures that the devotional content remains accessible to all users, irrespective of their visual capabilities.

Interactive Elements and Focus States

Interactive elements, including buttons and navigation items, were designed with distinct focus states. This not only aids users navigating through the app using keyboards or assistive technologies but also ensures a clear and intuitive user experience for individuals with motor impairments.

Functional Enhancements with Accessibility in Mind

The newly added functionalities, such as the bookmark feature, note-taking page, and voice reader, were developed with a focus on accessibility. For instance, the voice reader that enhances accessibility by providing an auditory experience for users, particularly those with visual impairments or those who prefer to consume content through audio
